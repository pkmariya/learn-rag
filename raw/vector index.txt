• Someone built a RAC vector index on Google's TurboQuant research, and it fits 10 million documents in just 4GB of database memory.
• The most common vector index that most RAC pipelines today run on is FICE, and before FICE can search anything, it needs to study your data first.
• It samples your documents, groups them into clusters, builds a look-up structure from that, and only then can you start searching.
• If your data grows or shifts significantly, you may need to redo that whole process.
• Google research published a paper last year called TurboQuant, which I covered a couple of months back, and it found something that changes this.
• If you apply a random rotation to your document vectors before compressing them, their statistical distribution becomes predictable.
• The math can figure out the shape of your data without ever seeing it.
• TurboWek is built on exactly that. You add a document and it gets indexed immediately.
• Your corpus can grow or shift without ever needing to rebuild the index.
• And the compression TurboQuant brings takes 10 million document embeddings from 31GB down to just 4.
• It's built in rust with Python wrappers for LAN chain, Lama index, and Haystack that are already there.
